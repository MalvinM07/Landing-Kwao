Web Development

01Landing Pages & Static Websites

The starting point of the web, focused mainly on presentation, marketing, and direct conversion. These pages show the same content to every visitor — the server simply delivers ready-made files (HTML/CSS) to the browser — with the only interaction usually being a simple contact or lead-capture form. No database or backend business logic required.

Examples: professional portfolios, product launch pages, simple company websites.

Complexity: Very Low
02Blogs & CMS-Powered Websites

Here dynamism comes into play: the site is no longer a static flyer but an updatable platform. Content changes frequently (news, articles) and an admin panel lets non-technical users create, edit, and publish pages without touching any code. Requires a database and backend logic to render pages dynamically.

Examples: news portals, corporate blogs, dynamic company websites.

Complexity: Low – Intermediate
03E-commerce Platforms

Systems built purely around commercial transactions and financial flows: managing product catalogs, inventory control, shopping carts, and payment processing. Security becomes a critical pillar, requiring encryption and compliance with financial data standards.

Examples: online retail stores, multi-vendor marketplaces.

Complexity: Intermediate
04Dynamic Web Apps & Portals

The focus shifts from "reading information" to "interacting intensively with tools and data." These are highly interactive systems, often built as Single-Page Applications (SPAs) — where the page never fully reloads — with advanced business rules and strict access control. Requires a robust backend API paired with a rich frontend that handles real-time data.

Examples: admin dashboards, online booking systems, internal CRMs or ERPs, and billing/business management systems.

Complexity: Advanced
